# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Two primary audiences, roughly equal weight. HR/admin staff (Level 1-2) run daily operations — managing employees, approving leave and overtime, processing payslips, tracking contracts and recruitment — desk-based, repetitive work where speed and low error rate matter most. Regular employees use the same system for self-service: checking their own leave balance, submitting requests, viewing payslips, uploading personal documents. Their use is occasional and needs to be foolproof without training, since most employees never read a manual before their first leave request.

## Product Purpose

Replaces manual and scattered HR processes — leave, overtime, payslips, contracts, and recruitment used to be handled across spreadsheets and ad hoc tools across Rhaya Group's multiple legal entities. Success looks like fewer errors, less manual reconciliation between systems, and faster approval cycles for both HR staff and employees.

## Positioning

One system for multi-entity HR operations, correctly scoped — not a generic single-company HR tool. It handles leave, overtime, payslips, contracts, and recruitment across many legal entities under one group, with HR access properly scoped per entity so Level-2 HR staff only ever see the employees and data within their assigned companies.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, efficient, no-nonsense. This is an internal ops tool handling sensitive data — payroll, contracts, leave records — so it should get out of the way and inspire confidence rather than reach for personality or delight. Every interaction should feel dependable: predictable patterns, clear state, no surprises.

## Anti-references

## Design Principles

- **Correctness over cleverness.** This system touches pay, leave entitlement, and contract dates — every interface decision should make the correct state obvious and the wrong action hard to take by accident (confirmations before destructive/high-stakes actions, explicit required fields, no ambiguous defaults).
- **Scope is not decoration.** Level-2 HR only sees their assigned entities; this boundary should be visually and structurally obvious everywhere data is scoped, not just enforced silently on the backend.
- **Serve two different rhythms of use.** HR staff live in this tool daily and need density and speed; employees drop in rarely and need clarity without a learning curve. The same components should work for both without compromising either.
- **Show your work.** Balances, approvals, and calculated fields (leave remaining, TOIL, overtime totals) should always be traceable to their source — never a number with no explanation of how it was derived.
- **Consistency across modules.** Leave, overtime, payslips, contracts, and recruitment are functionally separate but should feel like one system, not five bolted-together tools.
